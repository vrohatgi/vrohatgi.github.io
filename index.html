coder. builder. dreamer.

coder

I outgrew writing in diaries and moved onto writing blogs in junior high. Fiddling with my first Tumblr blog, I quickly learned HTML and CSS. My interest in web design inspired me to learn other languages, like Python, and I began writing programs that could do simple tasks, like calculate my grade. I discovered a universal tool that not only helped me develop my blog, but also allowed me to create anything I wanted. Now, I am fluent in Java, Javascript, Swift, and many more languages.

builder

After learning several languages, I ventured into hackathons my junior year. In my first competition, I created a web app, BusyBee, for my family to delegate chores. I was awarded Best App because my app solved a relatable real-world problem. By participating in hackathons, I learned how to code for a larger purpose than simply doing calculations. After winning first place at both PixelHacks and CodeDay, I discovered that through coding, I can make a difference. I also created another web app, called HealthyHarvest, and published an app, Wander, to the iTunes App Store. Feel free to view my code on Github.

dreamer

This year, I launched Design.it, a beginning coding club to encourage my classmates to learn a computer language and challenge themselves with a yearlong project. Coding had such a huge impact on me, and I want to help others discover its power. I am excited by its endless possibilities and capacity to solve problems we may not even know exist yet.
